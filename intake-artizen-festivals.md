## 3. Pitch framing patterns that already tested well (reuse in circuit outreach)

From the Artizen offer-optimization work. These beat generic pitches in the research:
- Place first, network second ("Ellsworth, the gateway to Acadia" outperformed "The ZAO presents")
- Proven-organizer proof block early (the table in section 1 IS this)
- Recurring-series framing: "Year N of the annual circuit," never a one-off ask
- Budget transparency: rough line-item split raises average commitment
- "What's at stake": tie dollars to outcomes ("$X books Y artists at living wages")
- Outbound style: max ~7 lines, ONE ask, no walls of text (Zaal house rule)
