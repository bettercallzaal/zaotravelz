## ENGAGEMENT PLAYBOOK: HOW CONFERENCES ACTIVATE OUTSIDE ENTERTAINMENT CREWS

### Standard 5-Format Side Event Architecture

Based on crypto conference best practices (applicable across music/tech/Web3):

1. **Summits** (200-600 attendees, 6-8 hours)
   - Requires: Defensible thesis, 4+ sponsors, speaker lineup
   - Budget: 10k-25k USD
   - Timing: Separate day or extended after-hours
   - Example: EthCC side summit on "DeFi + Music IP" (hypothetical)

2. **Builder Nights / Artist Showcases** (80-200 participants, 4-5 hours)
   - Requires: Developer/artist-focused infrastructure POV, working demos/live sets
   - Budget: 5k-10k USD
   - Timing: Evening slots, pre/post main conference
   - Example: "Live Music Onchain" coding workshop at ETHDenver

3. **VIP Dinners** (12-40 invitees, 3-4 hours)
   - Requires: Curated operator list (founders, artists, VCs); sponsors dilute effectiveness
   - Budget: 3k-8k USD
   - Timing: Private venue, exclusive access
   - Example: Closed networking dinner with artists + crypto builders

4. **After-Parties / Branded Evenings** (150-600 attendees, 4+ hours)
   - Requires: Branded evening format (DJ sets, live performances, ambient installations)
   - Budget: 10k-30k USD
   - Timing: Late evening (10pm-2am typical), held during main conference week
   - Example: ZAO Festivals multi-stage electronic music activation at Token2049

5. **Hackathon Workshops / Live Performance Stages** (40-150 developers + audience, 2-4 hours)
   - Requires: SDK/framework demo + bounty mechanism (or artist performance + fan engagement mechanics via NFT/tokens)
   - Budget: 5k-15k USD
   - Timing: Daytime or split sessions during hackathon windows
   - Example: "Build a Live Stream DAO" workshop + artist performance at EthCC

### Application & Outreach Timeline (T-45 to T-0)

**T-45 days:** Identify prospect sponsors (blockchain projects, music platforms, VCs, brands)
**T-40:** Send sponsor brief/deck (1-pager on activation concept + audience)
**T-38:** Conduct sponsor calls (pitch, confirm fit, discuss budget commitment)
**T-30:** Lock venue + secure verbal commitments from 3+ sponsors
**T-28:** Finalize speaker/artist lineup, confirm performance logistics
**T-21:** Contract finalized with sponsors; open Luma RSVP funnel
**T-14:** Confirm all logistics (AV, stage setup, bar/catering, WiFi)
**T-10:** Launch wave 1 amplification (warm outreach via Telegram, Discord, email)
**T-7:** Wave 2 amplification (announce artist names, keynotes, theme)
**T-5:** Sound check mandatory (prevent AV failures)
**T-3:** Wave 3 amplification (urgency messaging, 48-hr RSVP deadline)
**T-1:** Final headcount to caterers; confirm artist arrival times
**T+0:** Event execution
**T+7 to T+30:** Post-event cadence (recap videos, interviews, DAO voting on next activation, relationship maintenance)

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Thesis-First Mindset:** Define event in one sentence (e.g., "Live onchain music battles where artists compete and fans vote via tokens")
2. **Guest List > Catering:** 80% budget to speaker/artist fees + marketing; 20% to venue/bar
3. **Production Quality:** Crypto audiences expect professional AV, stage design, and lighting; execution mistakes damage brand
4. **30-Day Post-Event Cadence:** Most organizers skip this; post-event content, interviews, and relationship maintenance determines whether event compounds into reputation or stays one-off
5. **Avoid Venue Conflicts:** Don't host multiple events same week (burnout, audience fragmentation)
6. **Luma + Direct Outreach:** 3 promotional waves via email/Discord/Telegram; Luma handles RSVP tracking
